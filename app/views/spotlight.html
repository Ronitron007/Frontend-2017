Yoshua Bengio

DEEP LEARNING AND ARTIFICIAL INTELLIENCE
Yoshua Bengio is a renowned figure in the deep learning field. His titles include Full Professor of the Department of Computer Science & Operations Research at the Université de Montréal, head of the Machine Learning Laboratory (MILA), CIFAR Program Co-director of the CIFAR Neural Computation and Adaptive Perception program, and Canada Research Chair in Statistical Learning Algorithms, among many others. His current interests are centered around a quest for artificial intelligence, through machine learning, and include fundamental questions on deep learning and representation learning, the geometry of generalization in high-dimensional spaces, manifold learning, biologically inspired learning algorithms, and challenging applications of statistical machine learning.

Vijay Amritraj

A Fire Side Chat
Vijay Amritraj is a former Indian tennis player. He is one of the finest players of India and has been honoured with the Arjuna award and the Padmashree by the Indian Government. He is a multifacetted personality, having spent several years as a successful sports commentator and also as talk show host in CNN IBN. He had a breif career in Hollywood, starring in films like the Star trek IV and most noted for his role as MI alongside James Bond. He is also actively working for HIV awareness and prevention through The Vijay Amritraj foundation.

Peter Littlewood

SCIENCE OF SUSTAINABILITY ( A Fireside Chat )
Dr. Peter Littlewood is the director of Argonne National Laboratory. He received his PhD in physics from the University of Cambridge. He began his career in Bell Laboratories where he eventually became Head of the Theoretical Physics Research Department. He then moved to the Cavendish Laboratory in Cambridge University, becoming the department head in 2005. Dr. Littlewood has published more than 200 articles in scientific journals and has given more than 100 invited talks at international conferences, universities, and laboratories. He is also a fellow of the Royal Society of London, the Institute of Physics, The World Academy of Sciences, Trinity College Cambridge, and the American Physical Society. He works towards creating and understanding new materials and chemistries that will address the grand challenges in energy and the environment, and on discovery science across a broad spectrum of disciplines including nuclear and high-energy physics as well as accelerator physics and technology.

J Gopikrishnan

CORPORATISATION OF MEDIA AND MEDIA ETHICS ( A Fireside Chat )
J. Gopikrishnan is an Indian journalist known for exposing the infamous 2G telecom through his article in 2008. The report led to more than 200 articles being published by The Pioneer, which uncovered the massive 2G Spectrum and the related Aircel Maxis scams. He is the recipient of several distinguished awards in journalism like the Raman Goenka award , KC Kulish International Award and “Indian of the Year” from CNN-IBN. He is also an active member of several anti-corruption movements including Campaign for Judicial Accountability and Reform (CJAR) and Action Committee Against Corruption in India (ACACI).

Diwakar Vaish

BRAIN CLONING AND SUPER INTELLIGENCE
Diwakar Vaish is an Indian robotics researcher and Head of Robotics and Research at A-SET Training and Research Institutes. He is the developer of India's first completely indigenous 3D printed humanoid robot (Manav), India's first mind controlled robot, and world's first production brain controlled wheelchair. These technologies and robots were developed at the labs of A-SET Training & Research Institutes.

R Chidambaram

GLOBAL SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT: ROLE OF SCIENCE, TECHNOLOGY & INNOVATION ( A Fireside Chat )
Dr. R Chidambaram is one of the most renowned experimental physicists. Having received a PhD from Indian Institute of Science Bangalore, he started his glorious career at Bhabha Atomic Research Centre and served as its director from 1990. He also served as the director of Indian Atomic Energy Agency in for two years. He played a crucial role in coordinating the Historic Pokhran Nuclear tests, Smiling Buddha and Pokhran II in coordination with DRDO. Currently he is also the Chairman of the Scientific Advisory Committee to the Cabinet. A novel initiative for sustainable growth of the nation, RuTAG ( Rural Technology Action Group) , is his brainchild. The Government of India has conferred him with Padma Vibushan, the second highest civilian award, in 1999. He is also a member of various science academies India and abroad and holds twenty honorary doctorates to his credit.

Kartick Sathyanarayan

SAVING INDIA'S WILDLIFE
Kartick is a wildlife conservationalist, best known as the "Bear Man of India", for his efforts to eradicate the cruel tradition of bear dancing. He. along with his organisation Wildlife SOS have been working on taking action against animal cruelty, rescuing wildlife in distress, working to resolve man-animal conflicts while promoting and educating the public about the need for habitat protection. They are committed to working with communities who depend on wildlife for sustenance and providing them alternative and sustainable livelihoods as that is the key to sustainable conservation. Wildlife SOS also emphasizes the value of ex-situ research in veterinary areas, behavioural studies, reproductive physiology and other fields in collaboration with national and international universities and organisations.

Kasturirangan

SPACE EXPLORATION - INDIA'S JOURNEY ( A Fireside Chat )
Dr. Kasturirangan is a former Director of Indian Space Reasearch Organisation. He started his journey as as a post graduate in Physics from University of Mumbai. He went on to earn doctorate in Experimental High Energy Astronomy in 1971. During his tenure as the Director of ISRO, Indian space research achieved its key milestones in the development of PSLV and GSLV vehicles. He also headed the Indian Remote Sensing Satellites Projects IRS -1C and 1D. Being a scienctist in essence, he has published more than 200 papers in astronomy and space sciences. He serves on the Board of Governors of IIT Madras and as the Chairman of Karnataka Knowledge Commision. He is a Fellow of Indian Academy of Sciences and Indian National Academy of Engineering. The government of India awarded him the Padma Vibushan, the second highest civilian honour, in 2000.

Srijan Pal Singh

FURTURISTIC TECHNOLOGY ( A Fireside Chat )
Srijan Pal Singh is a young social entrepreneur cum author and the founder and director of DR. APJ Abdul Kalam Centre and Three Billion Initiative. He graduated as the best outgoing student from IIM Ahmedabad in 2009. After his stint as a management consultant at BCG, he joined hands with late Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam as the Officer on Special Duty and Advisor to the President. He has played a crucial role in the coordination of PURA-(Providing Urban Amenities to Rural Areas) and “What can I give” mission, the pilot projects of former President Abdul Kalam. He has co-authored three best-selling books including “Target 3 Billion” with Dr. Abdul Kalam. He is also the co-founder of Barefoot IT mission in Karnataka, to link rural people in need of facilities with the administration through smart technologies. He is a frequent speaker at various events like TEDx.

Srinivas Mohan

THE SCIENCE OF ILLUSIONS
Srinivas Mohan is an Indian visual effects designer. He is best known for unparalleled special effects creations like the Baahubali, Enthiran and the Magic Magic. He has also received the National Film Award for best special effects for all these films along with his work Sivaji-The boss. Besides these tamil movies, he has also worked in Kannada, Malayalam and Bollywood film industries. He also founded the company,Indian Artists Computer Graphics. He has been involved in training budding designers via numerous VFX training institutes throughout the country.
